## **PART 1: THE PORTAL LANDING PAGE**

**Purpose:** Allow visitors to choose between wedding videography and commercial/production work

### **Visual Design Concept**

**Layout:**
- Split screen with organic portal boundary (NOT a straight vertical line)
- LEFT: Wedding world
- RIGHT: Production world
- CENTER: Fluid, organic boundary/portal that shifts based on interaction

**Interaction Behavior:**

**Hover LEFT (Wedding Side):**
- Wedding side expands and begins to "consume" production side
- Portal boundary shifts smoothly to the right
- Production side compresses and dims
- Wedding footage becomes more prominent
- Elegant, romantic expansion

**Hover RIGHT (Production Side):**
- Production side expands and begins to "consume" wedding side
- Portal boundary shifts smoothly to the left
- Wedding side compresses and dims
- Commercial footage becomes more prominent
- Bold, dynamic expansion

**Neutral/Center State:**
- Balanced view of both worlds
- Organic portal boundary has subtle movement/glow
- Both sides equally visible
- Inviting exploration of either direction

**Click to Enter:**
- Chosen side expands fully
- Other side fades completely
- Transition animation begins (see below)

---

### **TRANSITION ANIMATIONS**

**TO WEDDING SITE (Left Side Click):**

**The Ring Flip Transition:**

1. **Ring appears from left side**
   - Wedding ring enters frame from left portal edge
   - Ring is flipping/spinning in 3D space
   - Tumbling motion, elegant rotation

2. **Ring moves to center and enlarges**
   - Ring smoothly animates to center of screen
   - Continues spinning while scaling up
   - Ring grows to fill majority of viewport

3. **Zoom into ring/diamond**
   - Camera/view zooms into the circular band or diamond
   - Round opening of ring becomes a circular mask
   - Everything outside the ring darkens/blurs

4. **Ring becomes portal**
   - The circular opening of the ring expands
   - Acts as a mask revealing the wedding website behind it
   - Zoom continues through the ring portal
   - Ring edges fade out as we pass "through" it
   - Wedding website fully revealed

5. **Complete transition**
   - Now fully in wedding website
   - Smooth, elegant, memorable entrance
   - Reinforces wedding/romance theme

**Technical Complexity:** HIGH
- Requires 3D animation (Lottie, Three.js, or WebGL)
- Complex masking and timing
- Performance optimization critical
- Fallback transition needed for slower connections

**Implementation Timeline:** Phase 3 (after both sites are built)

---

**TO PRODUCTION SITE (Right Side Click):**

**Simpler, Bolder Transition:**

- Fast cut or dynamic swipe
- Quick zoom into production footage
- More aggressive, less romantic
- Could be glitch effect, hard cut, or fast slide
- Reflects edgier production aesthetic

**Technical Complexity:** MEDIUM
- Simpler than ring animation
- Can use CSS transitions primarily
- Faster, more aggressive feel appropriate for this side

## **TECHNICAL CONSIDERATIONS**

**Performance:**

## **NOTES & REMINDERS**

**Philosophy:**
- Don't rush to build everything at once
- Wedding site is the revenue generator - prioritize it
- Production site can wait until wedding site is proven
- Portal is the "cherry on top" - fun but not essential initially
- Each piece should work independently before connecting

**The Ring Transition:**
- Cool idea, highly memorable
- Very complex to execute well
- Consider simpler alternatives first
- Can always upgrade later
- Don't let it delay the core sites

**Brand Name Decision:**
- Love, Violeta Rose = Romantic, distinctive, partner connection
- Weddings by Michael Andrade = Personal brand, direct, clear
- Decision impacts domain strategy and branding consistency
- Should be finalized before public launch
- Both work with the overall dual-site portal vision

**Keep It Simple:**
- Start with functional, beautiful basics
- Add complexity only when it serves the user experience
- Premium feel comes from refinement, not feature count
- Better to do simple things perfectly than complex things poorly
